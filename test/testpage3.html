This is a website that highlights the city of Seattle, Wa and provides other facts about the state of Washington.

Space Needle

The Space Needle is an iconic landmark that is in the heart of Seattle, Wa. It is a hop and skip away from the EMP museum and is a unique piece of architecture.

EMP

The EMP museum has interactive exibits that feature a wide variety of music. It was founded by Microsoft's co-founder Paul Allen in 2000.

Pike Place Market

Located right next to Elliot Bay, Pike Place Market opened August 17, 1907, and is one of the oldest continuously operated public farmers' markets in the United States. Tourists often like to go to the seafood section of the market that is known to have lively characters who like to throw seafood.

University of Wa.

The University of Washington is a premier university that is nestled in the northern most part of Seattle. It is thriving with young learners, scholars, entertainment, art, and so much more. The University of Washington is a part of the Pac-12 schools in college athletics.

Cascade Mountains

The Cascade Mountains are a beautiful mountain range that act as a dividing line between eastern and western Washington state. If you are an adventure seeker, then the Cascade Mountains are for you! In the winter time they have some great ski conditions and beautiful views of the valley from the tops of the mountain range.

Mount Rainier

Mount Rainer is a beautiful volcanic mountain that is the backdrop to the Seattle skyline. It stands at a whopping 14,411 ft. Many scientist often wonder when it is going to erupt. However, the local citizens continue to go on with life. Local Washingtonians have already lived through one eruption, Mount St. Helens.
